PROJECT OVERVIEW
    THE PRODUCT:
			
The AI-Based Competitive Exam Preparation App is an intelligent, scalable, and cost-effective digital learning solution that helps candidates with instant AI-driven doubt resolution, adaptive mock testing, and organized Previous Year Question (PYQ) practice. To ensure seamless and reliable operation for a broad user base, the product incorporates individualized performance statistics, secure student and administrator access, and a cloud-based architecture. The software improves exam readiness through self-paced and data-driven learning while remaining accessible, efficient, and cost-effective.
PROJECT TIMELINE: 11 WEEKS

PROBLEM STATEMENT
	
In today's competitive exam market, applicants confront a number of problems, including expensive coaching costs, a lack of individualized learning help, and difficulty tracking exam readiness. Most present platforms rely largely on live classes or generic material distribution, limiting accessibility and failing to properly address individual concerns. Furthermore, students frequently struggle to recognize their weak points and practice effectively, resulting in poor preparation outcomes. The suggested AI-powered app intends to address these issues by offering affordable, self-paced, and tailored exam preparation that includes fast doubt resolution, adaptive mock testing, and performance statistics.

USABILITY STUDIES
Round 01 – LLD Generation Confusion
●The initial idea-to-LLD screen showed too many options at once (Q&A input, chatbot edit, export, save, history, tech stack), which confused first-time users.
●Participants struggled to identify the main action—starting the guided Q&A—because many buttons appeared with equal importance.
●
Recommendation:
 Simplify the entry screen by highlighting the main “Start Q&A” action and grouping other features (history, export, settings, saved projects) under menus for a cleaner, focused experience.
Round 02 – Feature Accessibility Improvement
●After simplifying the interface, users wanted faster access to saved projects, previous LLD versions, and investor-shared project packages.
●Users also requested more control over how architecture is shown, including diagram view, text explanation, tech stack view, and investor-ready summaries.
●
Recommendation:
 Add customizable LLD views and quick-access icons for Saved Projects, Versions, Tech Stack, and Investor Requests to improve navigation speed and support better idea sharing.
